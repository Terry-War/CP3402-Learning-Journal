# Week 08 - learning Journal - Learning Experiment
## Learning Activities & Resources
This week I conducted a learning experiment to test whether handwritten notes are more effective than typed notes for remembering content.

### Hypothesis
If I take handwritten notes instead of typing them, then I will remember more information from the topic.

### Test
To test this hypothesis, I selected two similar topics from my course content.

For the first topic, I took handwritten notes for approximately 20 minutes, focusing on summarising key points in my own words.

For the second topic, I took typed notes for the same amount of time, using my laptop to record the same type of information.

After completing both study sessions, I waited a short period and then wrote down as many key points as I could remember from each topic without looking at my notes.

I then compared how much information I was able to recall from each method.

### Results
The handwritten notes resulted in better recall. I was able to remember more key points and explain the topic more clearly compared to the typed notes.

The typed notes were faster to create, but I found that I was more likely to copy information directly rather than process it. Writing by hand forced me to think more about what I was writing, which improved my understanding.

These results support my hypothesis that handwritten notes are more effective for my learning.

## Content Insights
This experiment showed that different note-taking methods can significantly impact how well information is retained. While typing notes is more efficient, it does not always lead to better understanding.

Handwriting notes slowed the process down, but this actually helped with processing and remembering the information. It encouraged me to summarise rather than copy content directly.

## Career
Being able to identify effective learning methods is important for ongoing professional development. In many careers, especially in IT, it is necessary to learn new technologies quickly, so understanding how I learn best will be valuable in the long term.

## Employability
This experiment demonstrates the ability to reflect on learning strategies and improve personal performance. These skills are important for employability, as they show initiative, adaptability, and a willingness to improve.

## Learning insights
The main insight I gained is that slower, more active learning methods can be more effective than faster, passive ones. Even though typing notes feels more efficient, it does not always lead to better retention.

In future, I will aim to use handwritten notes for more complex topics where understanding is important, and use typed notes for quicker tasks or organisation.
